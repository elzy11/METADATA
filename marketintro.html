~/metaphors/01_attention_market/intro
metaphor
engagement as stock — every like, comment and share is a trade, and your attention is the asset being priced. Raw material.
Wall Street values Meta in dollars; the feed values you in engagement. Two markets, one company, one closes every night, the other never sleeps.
This is what they publish, what they admit to - the evidence. Concrete numbers: engagement rates, revenue per minute, the financial result of designed behaviour. Architecture as law — the technical choices that make extraction not just possible but invisible.
description

A live trading terminal with two charts for the same company. On top, the attention market: engagement per minute in the EU, drawn as candlesticks that breathe with the day and tick every second. Below it, the money: META on NASDAQ, real closing prices from the 2012 IPO to a live quote. Zoom from one day to fourteen years — and watch the moments where the two lines disagree: the money panics, the attention never flinches.
