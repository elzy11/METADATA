HOW TO READ THIS
Above ground: people on Facebook & Instagram right now. One node = 250,000 users online; the count follows the daily and weekly rhythm and updates each minute (±3% minute-to-minute variance is simulated). Leaves are engagement events — likes, comments, shares, posts — appearing at the per-user rate of the year shown; that rate has fallen about 8× since 2012.
Below ground: the root fabric represents all revenue Meta has ever earned — about $6.9B entering 2012, over $1.1 trillion today. It only ever grows; in LIVE mode it keeps growing at the real revenue rate.
The moving light is passive data collection, which operates independently of engagement; it has run since the late 2000s and its volume here scales with revenue. Drag the timeline to any point between 2012 and today. $/€ converts at 0.877 (July 2026).
