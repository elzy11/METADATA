metaphor
growth as a tree — the tree is the activity we see: people online, posting, liking, sharing. The roots are the value we never see: the system operating quietly, feeding from that activity, accumulating underground.
The tree grows with its users.
The roots grow with what its users are worth.
"You can't resist what you can't perceive." — Shoshana Zuboff, The Age of Surveillance Capitalism

The mechanism isn't just code, it is a specific economic logic that converts human experience into a commodity. Data flows downward whether or not you notice. Profit accumulates underground while the visible surface looks like a fair exchange.
description

Above ground: what we see
The tree represents activity — people on Facebook and Instagram right now. One node = 250,000 users online; the count follows the real daily and weekly rhythm and updates each minute. Leaves are engagement events — likes, comments, shares, posts — appearing at the per-user rate of the year shown, with real weekly and seasonal variation. That rate has fallen about 8× since 2012.

Below ground: what we do not see
The roots represent all advertising revenue Meta has ever earned, the value extracted from attention and data, approximately $5.8B entering 2012, over $1 trillion today. It only keeps growing and spreading.

The moving light, the energy travelling through the roots — is passive data collection. It runs whether anyone engages or not. It has run since the late 2000s. It does not need your click.
In LIVE mode it grows at the real ad-revenue rate.
