what it means
~/metaphors/03_space/intro
metaphor
You are a star. You orbit the black hole. You have no perspective on the system from inside it. The black hole doesn't negotiate. Gravity is just the condition of your existence.
The galaxy is not a metaphor for the platform - it is the platform, rendered at its actual scale. This is the condition of human existence in the digital age — not a choice you made, but a system you are in.
description

Each star is a real quantity of people using Facebook or Instagram right now. They appear at the edge of the galaxy, drifting inward, vanishing as sessions end. They follow the platform's real daily and weekly rhythm.

Larger stars represent one person's full session, which you can actively follow. They light up pink when they engage with the platform individually. Smaller stars are multiple users, that have cyan sparks representing engagement collectively. (10,000 engagements in a single second across multiple users)

The black hole at the center grows with every dollar earned in ad revenue since 2004. It does not pulse or react. It simply grows. And everything orbits it.
